Research Interests

I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Climate Studies at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IITB), in the Climate Change and Climate Variability Lab, under the guidance of Prof. Angshuman Modak. My research broadly focuses on how interactions between the ocean and atmosphere, especially through radiative feedbacks, shape the tropical climate.

I use a combination of observational datasets and climate models to understand the physical processes that drive tropical climate variability and change.

Ph.D. Topic – Pattern Effect & Tropical Climate Response

The evolution of global sea surface temperature (SST) patterns plays a central role in determining how the Earth’s climate responds to greenhouse gas forcing. These spatial SST variations cause differences in radiative feedbacks—a phenomenon known as the pattern-effect—which strongly modulate both global and regional climate responses.

My Ph.D. explores how these SST pattern variations influence tropical precipitation and atmospheric circulation, with the broader goal of improving near-term projections of tropical climate.

M.Sc. Dissertation – Seasonal Prediction of Cyclonic Disturbances

My M.Sc. dissertation focused on developing a statistical–machine learning hybrid framework to predict seasonal cyclonic disturbances over the Bay of Bengal. I identified key predictors such as SST anomalies, vertical wind shear, and mid-tropospheric humidity, using multiple linear regression and decision trees to forecast CD frequency.

Broader Research Interests

Tropical Climate Dynamics & Internal Variability

I am intrigued by how large-scale systems like the Walker circulation respond to warming. This includes exploring how radiative feedbacks and ocean-atmosphere coupling shape vertical motion, cloud feedbacks, and precipitation across the tropics.

Key References:
Held & Soden (2006), Vecchi & Soden (2007), Watanabe et al. (2023)

Extratropical–Tropical Interactions

I explore how high-latitude climate changes like Arctic amplification or Southern Ocean anomalies influence tropical convection and rainfall. Mechanisms include energy redistribution and Rossby wave teleconnections.

Key References:
Kang et al. (2008), Chiang & Friedman (2012)

Teleconnections and Indian Climate

I am interested in how modes like ENSO, IOD, Atlantic Niño, and PDV jointly influence the Indian monsoon through nonlinear teleconnections. Understanding these links is key to improving monsoon prediction and regional projections.

Key References:
Ashok et al. (2007), Huang et al. (2020), Kucharski et al. (2007)
